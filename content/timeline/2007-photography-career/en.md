---
title: "Photographer and freelance work"
summary: "Before software engineering, I worked for many years as a photographer, including staff work for a glossy magazine and freelance client projects."
---

From 2007 to 2022, I worked as a photographer. Part of that path was in a glossy magazine as a staff photographer, and a much larger part was freelance work with clients, deadlines, locations, and responsibility for the final result.

In the context of software engineering, this experience matters more than it may seem at first. Freelance photography taught me to work independently, understand client expectations, plan delivery, take responsibility, and keep moving when there is no manager telling me every next step.

In Finland, I also had experience working with companies and organizations such as Škoda Transtech and the City of Joensuu. Those projects helped me understand how important communication, trust, and reliability are when the work is visible to real people.

In 2022, I decided to change profession. Photography is often freelance and self-employed work, with few stable full-time positions. It also requires a lot of movement and physical presence. I wanted an interesting profession where I could build more long-term skills, work with complex systems, and grow inside a field with clearer employment opportunities.

Software engineering felt like a natural return to an old interest. I had been interested in computers for a long time, and my first HTML book appeared in the early 2000s. Back then, it did not feel as accessible or as clearly connected to education and work. Years later, after studying and practicing development, I understand that this direction fits me and that I genuinely enjoy it.
