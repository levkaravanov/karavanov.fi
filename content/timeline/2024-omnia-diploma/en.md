On October 18, 2024, I received my Omnia diploma: Tieto- ja viestintätekniikan perustutkinto, 180 osp, with the qualification title Ohjelmistokehittäjä.

For me, this was more than a formal certificate. Omnia was my first major education step in Finland: a place to strengthen my Finnish, understand the Finnish education system, and build a practical software development foundation before moving on to Metropolia.

The vocational software-related part was 145 osp, and I completed every professional development module with a 5/5 grade: ICT fundamentals, programming, working as a software developer, and implementing software with component libraries.

The studies covered Python, SQL and relational databases, HTML/CSS, PHP, Bootstrap, JavaScript basics, Git, APIs, libraries, debugging, testing, security basics, UML, data structures, and software architecture. It was a broad introduction that helped me move from being interested in programming toward a more intentional engineering path.
