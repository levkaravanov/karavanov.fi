On June 27–30, 2026, I built this website and brought it to its first public version. The whole process happened in Codex: reference research, PRD, layout work, interface revisions, real content, domain purchase, Firebase Hosting deploy, and SEO setup.

The first day started with the idea, the structure, and the documentation. I wanted a way to show my path into software development by year: education, practice, projects, tools, and important turns inside one timeline. References helped me explain the structure to Codex more clearly, and after that the stack, PRD, and first placeholder layout were shaped around the idea.

On the second day, the work moved into polishing the layout and filling it with content. The site was built with Next.js, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS. The colors come from my DailyHero project, and orange became the main accent also because it is my favorite color. During this stage, I iterated on the hero section, timeline, themes, languages, cards, and detail views.

The third day again went into polish and content. I kept writing texts about education, work practice, projects, freelance work, DailyHero, and the transition from photography to software development. At the same time, I kept polishing the interface, bought the karavanov.fi domain, and deployed the site to Firebase Hosting.

On the fourth day, I continued the polish: interface details, links, public sharing, and social media posts about building this website. I also worked on SEO: metadata, sitemap, robots.txt, social previews, and submitting the site to search engines.

For me, this website is an example of an AI-assisted workflow. I set the task, made decisions, checked the result, edited the wording, and gradually brought the idea to a working product. Codex was the main development tool, while the direction, structure, and final decisions stayed with me.

Localization is a separate part of the project. The site is built in three languages from the beginning: Russian, Finnish, and English. Russian is my native language, Finnish connects to my life and studies in Finland, and English is necessary for software development and international work.
