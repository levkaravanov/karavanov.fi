On June 27–28, 2026, I built the first working version of this website. The entire development process happened in Codex: from reference research and structure discussions to implementation, design revisions, and content work.

I started with clear search criteria. I was not just looking for a beautiful developer portfolio, but for a way to show my path by year. The references helped me explain the idea to AI more precisely: a short intro, then a timeline where education, internships, projects, and tools live inside one story.

After that, I defined the stack and wrote the PRD around the idea. The site is built with Next.js, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS. The colors come from my DailyHero project, and orange became the main accent also because it is my favorite color.

The first version was a layout with placeholders. Then I iterated on it through my own comments: the hero structure, timeline behavior, themes, languages, videos, cards, and smaller interface details.

The next day, I started replacing placeholders with real content: texts about education, internships, projects, freelance work, and the transition from photography to software development.

For me, this website is also an example of an AI-assisted workflow. It is not a website simply made by AI, but a project where I set the task, made decisions, checked the result, edited the wording, and gradually brought the idea to a working product.

Localization is a separate part of the project. The site is built in three languages from the beginning: Russian, Finnish, and English. Russian is my native language, Finnish connects to my life and studies in Finland, and English is necessary for software development and international work.
